HR Analytics Dashboard - Key Business Insights

* Research & Development department recorded the highest employee attrition count.
* Employees working overtime showed significantly higher attrition compared to employees not working overtime.
* Employees in lower salary slabs experienced higher attrition rates.
* Male employees contributed a larger share of total attrition due to higher workforce representation.
* Employee attrition was highest during the early years of employment.
* Sales Representatives experienced one of the highest attrition levels among job roles.
* Laboratory Technicians also showed relatively high employee turnover.
* Younger employees contributed significantly to overall attrition.
* Attrition patterns varied across different education fields.
* Employees with lower job satisfaction levels showed higher attrition.
* Job satisfaction appeared to be an important factor influencing employee retention.
* Employee retention improved among employees with longer tenure in the organization.


Recommendations:-

* Reduce excessive overtime and improve work-life balance.
* Review compensation strategies for lower salary groups.
* Strengthen employee engagement during the first few years of employment.
* Develop retention programs for high-attrition job roles.
* Improve job satisfaction through career development and recognition initiatives.
* Monitor department-level attrition trends and take corrective actions where necessary.
